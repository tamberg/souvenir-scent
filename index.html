Learn more about the science of olive scent.

"The aroma profile of olives becomes richer thanks to inoculation and fermentation. Alcohols, aldehydes, ketones, esters as well as acids make up the volatile organic compounds found in fermented olives. Some molecules that give olives its distinct scent are Ethyl acetate, 2-butanone and 2-pentanol." ‐ Sabatini et al. 2017

Learn more about the science of seafood scent.

"The smell of the Sea comes from volatile organic compounds that contain Sulphur. These compounds are produced by decomposing seaweed, like Hydrogen Sulfide or Dimethylsulfoniopropionate, which is a compound found in algae cells, when broken down by bacteria creates DMS, which is a major component of the sea smell." ‐ Source
